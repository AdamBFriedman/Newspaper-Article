Florida man moves to San Francisco to begin a new journey; discovers JavaScript

Sometimes your life just changes. One day you're on the business path of Accounting and Project Management, and then BOOM - a career change happens! Well, this happened to a young front-end developer who fell into the world of tech by chance... and never looked back.

The developer, whose work includes a COVID-19 Tracker, clarified, "Well, I didn't mean to get into web development, it just sort of happened". The company I was working for, he said, needed their website redone and I was curious about what that process would look like. After taking a month to learn the ins and outs of HTML/CSS, the developer moved onto learning JavaScript.

"I don't know, it just sort of came to me. I understood it." he told Google during his one of his onsite interviews. Google, whose corporate mission is to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful, declined to comment.

Brady Bunch or Friedman Bunch?

The Brady Friedman Bunch is an American sitcom created by Sherwood Schwartz that aired from September 26, 1969, to March 8, 1974, on ABC. The series revolves around a large blended family with six children. Considered one of the last of the old-style family sitcoms, the series aired for five seasons and, after its cancellation in 1974, went into syndication in September 1975.

Are you having trouble sorting arrays?

Explore one of the fastest sorting algorithms in existence. Merge Sort is a Divide and Conquer algorithm that divides the input array into two halves, calls itself for the two halves and then merges the sorted halves. The Big O complexity for this sorting method is O(n*log(n)) which barrels through its competition (quick, heap, insertion, and bubble).
